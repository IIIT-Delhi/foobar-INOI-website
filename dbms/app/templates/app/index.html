Workshop for Teachers of Computer Science in Senior Secondary Schools (11th and 12th)

1st August, 8th August and 16th August 2015



About the Workshop

CS is one of the popular subjects in science stream in high school in India. The syllabus for class XII is mostly around the language features (C++, and now also Python) and Object Orientation. Teaching quality can be improved if programming is taught as a problem solving approach using simple data structures and algorithms (and using them for suitable projects), which is supported with some effective approaches for programming and debugging. The goal of this workshop is to enhance the background of school teachers for strengthening their CS teaching. Another goal of this workshop is build a community of school teachers for ongoing discussion for continued improvement of CS education in schools. The workshop will be held at IIIT-Delhi, a premiere Institute in Delhi in CS/IT, and will be conducted by CS faculty from IIIT-Delhi, IIT Delhi, Delhi University, Google, and some well-known teachers from schools. The faculty will be supported by advanced students from IIIT-Delhi for conducting labs.

Registration fees are waived for the participants thanks to a grant from Google. However, Registration is mandatory and seats are limited and will be on a first come first served basis. The registrations will open on 10th July 2015.
Completion certificates will be given to those who attend all days of the workshop.

    Eligibility

    Teachers in any school in Delhi-NCR can apply. Each school can send a list of upto 3 faculty members which has to be sent either by the Principal or the Computer Science instructor (in decreasing order of priority). Number of seats for the workshop will be limited to 150. If there are more nominations, then the number of teachers taken from a school may be reduced.
